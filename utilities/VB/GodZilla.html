Care Mistakes

How many care mistakes are required to evolve. The number of full skulls indicates how many care mistakes are required, while half skulls indicate that a care mistake is optional. In the example to the left, 2 care mistakes are required, and the half skull means 3 care mistakes will work as well, though 4 will not. For evolving into Stage V, VI or VI+; more than 4 care mistakes will immediately kill your Monster when it is time for it to evolve.

G-Cells

The Maximum or Minimum of G-Cells required. If it says Max, then the meter shown is how high you can go before the evolution route changes. Inversely, if it says Min then that's the point to which you must increase the meter to obtain that evolution. Remember that not all of a specific color's G-Cells will be visible as the meter levels up, so if you are showing a meter with 4 Yellow and 5 Red G-Cells for example, this also means that you have 14 Blue G-Cells even though you cannot see them. Please review the G-Cell section of the manual for full details.

G-Cell Fragment

Indicates that the current Monster must have been hatched from a G-Cell Fragment. Please review the G-Cell Fragments section of the manual for full details.

50% Chance

Chibi Godzilla and Chibi Mecha Godzilla have the same evolution requirements, so which one you get when meeting those requirements is random. You have a 50 percent chance of getting either one.

Area Clear

Certain requirements will only be possible if Area 7 of Quest Mode has been cleared or not cleared. If a requirement doesn't mention anything about Areas, then it does not matter what Areas you have cleared.

Other Conditions Not Met

If you raise a Digimon that doesn't meet the requirements for other evolutions, it will follow this path. For example, if you have Biollante, and you get 0 care mistakes, but you have fewer than 10 Yellow G-Cells, you will get Zilla. You don't get Space Godzilla since that require 0-2 Care Mistakes AND at least 10 Yellow G-Cells.

Fulfill Battles

This indicates that your Digimon needs to battle at least 15 times in its current form, and that it must have an 80% Win Ratio for a 100% chance to evolve. If you have 60% or higher, but less than 80%, you will have a 50% chance of evolution. If you have 40% or higher, but less than 60%, you will have a 25% chance of evolution. You will not be able to evolve if your Win Ratio is less than 40%.

If you aren't sure about something, take a minute and read throught the questions below, you may find your answer there. If not, try asking in the #help_and_questions channel at the Digitama Hatchery Discord Server.
  • What counts as a care mistake?
    • A care mistake occurs any time the Call Light goes out on its own without the Monster's need being take care of. Your Monster will call when its Hunger or Strength Hearts empty, or when it is time for it to go to bed. You have 10 minutes to refill hearts and 1 hour to put a Monster to bed before the Call Light goes out and you get a Care Mistake. If Hunger and Strength empty at the same time, each will count as its own care mistake, even if the Call Light goes out at the same time for both. If an empty meter is not filled after the call light goes out on its own, your Monster will call again when its hunger or loss timer next ends. Poop, injury, overfeeding and waking a sleeping or tired Monster do not count as Care Mistakes.
  • How do I get Effort Hearts?
    • Every four times you Train, you will get an Effort Heart. It does not matter if the Training is successful, it will count towards evolution and your current Effort Heart total. Please note that Effort Hearts are entirely there for aesthetic purposes, and some evolutions will require training more times than the Effort Hearts will display.
  • How long will it take my Monster to evolve?
    • Current Stage Time to Next Stage
    I 10 Minutes
    II 12 Hours
    III 24 Hours
    IV 36 Hours
    V 48 Hours
    VI 48 Hours
    VI+ N/A
    Unless you kill it first, your Monster is guaranteed to evolve into Stage IV. If a Stage IV, V or VI stage Monster has fulfilled its specific requirements for reaching Stage V, VI or VI+ respecitvely, it is guaranteed to reach that stage. The time your Monster spends sleeping also counts towards its evolution timer. The times above are the official times from Bandai, but you may experience minor variances.
  • How the heck does the G-Cell Meter work?
    • This is a pretty big conversation, too big for this FAQ. Head to this section of the manual for all the details.
  • What carries over after evolution?
    • Win Ratio and G-Cells will carry over after evolution. Care Mistakes, Injuries and the number of Battles for evolution all reset upon evolution.
  • A requirement doesn't mention a specific stat, how do I know how much of that stat I need?
    • The evolution requirements will only show stats are are actually required, hence them being called that. Not all stats are required for evolving, and if a specific stat isn't listed, this means it does not matter at all. For example, Battles are not required for evolving from Stage III, so a number of battles is not listed.
  • What does it mean if the evolution requirements on this guide show multiple bullet points?
    • When you click on a Monster in the guide, sometimes you will see bullet points listing multiple requirements. In these cases, you only need to choose one of the bullet points, as these are all independent requirements. For example, Space Godzilla has two bullet points for evolving into Destoroyah. One reads "0-1 Care Mistakes, 0-9 Red G-Cells*, 15+ Battles, 80%+ Win Ratio*" while the other reads "2-3 Care Mistakes, 15+ Battles, 80%+ Win Ratio*". You will meet the requirement by fulfilling either of those conditions upon reaching the evolution window (In this case it would also be impossible to fulfill both bullet points at once, since you cannot have 0-1 Care Mistakes AND 2-3 Care Mistakes at the same time).
  • When a requirement says I need a specific number of battles, do I need to win those battles?
    • No, all battles will work regardless of whether you win or lose. Requirements will specifically mention victories if they are necessary.
  • If I did 15 battles at Stage III or IV, do I have to do 15 more at Stage IV or V to evolve into Stage V or VI?
    • Yes, battles for each evolution must be done at the current stage.
  • When evolving, are only a certain number of battles counted toward Win Ratio?
    • No, the displayed Win Ratio in your stats is the exact number used. This number is the win ratio across all battles for your Monster, regardless of the evolutionary stage in which those battles were held. This is in opposition to how it is handled on the Original Digital Monster or the Digital Monster Ver.20th, where only the last 15 battles are factored in for Win Ratio.
  • Can I still evolve into Stage V or VI with a Win Ratio less than 80%?
    • Yes, but it isn't guaranteed. A Win Ratio of 80% or higher results in a 100% chance of evolution, assuming you have fulfilled the other requirements for that Monster. If you have 60% or higher, but less than 80%, you will have a 50% chance of evolution. If you have 40% or higher, but less than 60%, you will have a 25% chance of evolution. You will not be able to evolve if your Win Ratio is less than 40%.
  • What types of battles count for evolution?
    • Both Quest Mode and Connection Battles count. Every individual round of an area in Quest Mode is 1 battle, so clearing Area 1 for example is 3 battles.
  • Is there a chance my Monster won't evolve?
    • As long as you don't kill it first, your Monster will evolve into Stage IV. If you fail to meet the requirements for evolving into Stage V or VI before the evolution timer runs out, that Monster will never evolve.
  • How do I get a traited egg?
    • Upon death, there is a 30% chance that you will receive a traited egg for most Monsters that are at or above Stage V. This will increase the power of the Monster that hatches from it by a specific amount depending on its current evolutionary stage, see the manual page for details.
  • How do I get a G-Cell Fragment?
    • Certain monsters when getting a Traited Egg will have a 50% chance of getting a G-Cell fragment instead. See the manual page for details.
  • What will cause my Monster to die?
    • Getting injured 15 times in one form
    • Remaining injured for 6 hours consecutive in one form
    • Leaving the Hunger or Strength hearts empty for 12 consecutive hours in one form
    • Having 5 or more Care Mistakes when or after the evolution timer for your Stage IV or V Monster runs out without having fulfilled the requirements for evolution
    • Having 5 or more Care Mistakes when or after your Stage VI or VI+ Monster has been in that form for 48 hours

  • Can the Digital Monster Color connect to older two-pronged V-Pets?
    • No, the Digital Monster Color can only battle against other devices in the Digital Monster Color series, Digimon Pendulum Color series and Dgivice Color series.
